Dictionary of Rush Terms

  • Rush: the time of the year dedicated to running events with the intention of recruiting new members
  • Rush Chairs: the (usually 2) people who are in charge of making rush happen
  • Rushees: people who come over to the house during rush who are candidates for being future ET members
  • Bid: a formal invitation to join the house
  • Bidder: the person assigned to give a bid to a rushee
  • Pledge: someone who has accepted their bid and is now an active
  • Filing: a vote by an ET active on whether we should admit a particular rushee to ET. These indicate that you have interact with a particular rushee enough to have opinions on them. They are subdivided into:
    • Bid: I think this person should be given a bid
    • No Objection: I don't have an objection to this person being given a bid
    • Weak: I have an objection to this person being given a bid. This person will not be given a bid without resolving your objection
    • Flush: this person should not be allowed to be at our rush events. This indicates a serious concern needing immediate resolution that doesn't come up very frequently
